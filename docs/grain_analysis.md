# Grain Analysis

## Purpose

Before designing the Business Silver Layer, Gold Layer, PostgreSQL Warehouse, and Power BI dashboards, it is essential to understand the true business grain of every dataset. The Datagami problem statement explicitly states that `invoice_no` should not be assumed to be the primary key. Therefore, all modelling decisions in this project are based on evidence collected from the profiled Silver datasets rather than assumptions.

The objectives of this document are to:

- Determine the business grain of every dataset.
- Understand what a single row represents.
- Identify relationships between datasets.
- Determine future fact and dimension tables.
- Provide evidence-based modelling decisions.
- Establish the architectural foundation for Business Silver and the warehouse.

---

# Dataset 1 – Order Summary Report

## Dataset Overview

The Order Summary Report contains order-level transactional information generated by the Restaurant POS system. Each row stores financial details, timestamps, payment information, order status and other business attributes associated with a customer order.

### Profiler Summary

**May 2026**

- Rows: 31,324
- Columns: 31
- Duplicate Rows: 0

**June 2026**

- Rows: 30,514
- Columns: 31
- Duplicate Rows: 0

---

## Business Purpose

This dataset represents the financial view of restaurant operations and will become the primary source for order-level analytical reporting.

Typical business questions answered include:

- Daily sales
- Revenue trends
- Order counts
- Payment mix
- Order status
- Gross sales metrics

---

## Business Grain

### One row represents one order-level business transaction.

Each record contains:

- Order timestamp
- Payment information
- Order status
- Order type
- Financial measures
- Restaurant and location information

The dataset therefore represents an order transaction rather than individual products, customers or kitchen events.

---

## Evidence

The dataset contains order-level financial measures including:

- my_amount
- total_tax
- discount
- delivery_charge
- container_charge
- total

Only one value exists for:

- payment_type
- order_type
- status

These characteristics confirm an order-level business grain.

---

## Business Key Status

The profiler shows:

**May 2026**

- Rows: 31,324
- Distinct Invoice Numbers: 51.57%

**June 2026**

- Rows: 30,514
- Distinct Invoice Numbers: 58.30%

This confirms that `invoice_no` is **not unique**.

Therefore, invoice_no cannot be treated as the primary key.

At this stage no business key is assumed.

The final business key will be determined after validating relationships across all datasets during warehouse modelling.

---

## Measures

The following columns will become measures in the future FactOrders table.

- my_amount
- total_tax
- discount
- delivery_charge
- container_charge
- round_off
- total

These measures support order-level analytical reporting.

---

## Candidate Dimensions

The dataset naturally connects to:

- Date
- Brand
- Platform
- Area
- Payment Type
- Order Type
- Order Status

Brand and Platform are currently embedded inside `sub_order_type` and will be extracted during Business Silver.

---

## Business Silver Responsibilities

The following business transformations will be performed during Business Silver.

- Brand extraction
- Platform extraction
- Brand standardization
- Platform normalization
- Business Date derivation
- Weekday derivation
- Month derivation
- Quarter derivation
- Daypart derivation

These transformations intentionally belong in Business Silver because they are Restaurant POS specific rather than generic data quality improvements.

---

## Low Business Value Columns

The profiler indicates that several columns contain no meaningful analytical information.

- gst_no (100% null)
- assign_to (100% null)
- customer_phone (100% null)
- customer_address (100% null)
- customer_locality (100% null)

These columns will remain available for lineage but will not be modelled as warehouse dimensions.

---

## Constant Columns

The following columns contain constant zero values throughout the analysed datasets.

- service_charge
- additional_charge
- deduction_charge
- waived_off

These fields provide little analytical value and are unlikely to appear in analytical marts.

---

## Relationship

Order Summary represents the parent business transaction.

Relationship:

One Order

↓

Many Order Items

The dataset will therefore become the foundation of the future FactOrders table.

---

## Conclusion

The Order Summary dataset is confirmed to represent order-level business transactions.

It provides the financial measures required for analytical reporting.

The dataset establishes the business grain for FactOrders while intentionally postponing business key selection until warehouse modelling.

# Dataset 2 – Order Summary Item Report

## Dataset Overview

The Order Summary Item Report contains item-level transactional information for every order generated by the Restaurant POS system. Unlike the Order Summary Report, which stores one record per order-level transaction, this dataset stores one record for each menu item sold within an order.

Each row contains product-specific information such as the item name, category, quantity, price and total value, making this dataset suitable for detailed menu and product analysis.

### Profiler Summary

**May 2026**

- Rows: 56,154
- Columns: 32
- Duplicate Rows: 0

**June 2026**

- Rows: 53,274
- Columns: 32
- Duplicate Rows: 0

---

## Business Purpose

This dataset captures detailed product sales for every customer order.

Typical business questions answered include:

- Which menu items generate the highest revenue?
- Which categories sell the most?
- What quantities of each item are sold?
- Which products perform best across brands and platforms?

This dataset provides the detailed analytical view that complements the order-level information stored in the Order Summary Report.

---

## Business Grain

### One row represents one menu item sold within a customer order.

Unlike the Order Summary dataset, multiple records may exist for the same order because a single order can contain multiple menu items.

Therefore, this dataset represents **item-level transactions** rather than complete customer orders.

---

## Evidence

The dataset contains attributes that exist only at the item level.

Examples include:

- item_name
- category_name
- item_price
- item_quantity
- item_total

The profiler also shows that invoice numbers repeat frequently.

**May 2026**

- Rows: 56,154
- Distinct Invoice Numbers: 30.48%

**June 2026**

- Rows: 53,274
- Distinct Invoice Numbers: 33.41%

This demonstrates that multiple rows belong to the same customer order, confirming an item-level business grain.

---

## Business Key Status

The profiler confirms that `invoice_no` alone cannot uniquely identify an item-level record.

At this stage, no business key is assumed.

The final business key will be determined after validating relationships across all datasets during warehouse modelling.

Avoiding assumptions at this stage aligns with the assignment requirement to discover business grain before designing the warehouse.

---

## Measures

The following fields will become measures within the future FactOrderItems table.

- item_quantity
- item_price
- item_total

These measures support detailed product-level sales analysis.

---

## Candidate Dimensions

This dataset naturally connects to the following business dimensions.

- Date
- Item
- Category
- Brand
- Platform
- Order Type
- Payment Type

Brand and Platform are inherited from the parent order and will be standardized during the Business Silver phase.

---

## Business Silver Responsibilities

Business Silver will enrich this dataset by introducing business-specific attributes such as:

- Standardized Brand
- Standardized Platform
- Business Date
- Weekday
- Month
- Quarter
- Daypart

No business transformations are performed within Generic Silver in order to preserve the separation between generic data quality improvements and Restaurant POS business logic.

---

## Relationship

Relationship with Order Summary:

```
One Order
      │
      ▼
Many Order Items
```

Each order may contain multiple menu items.

This establishes a one-to-many relationship that forms the foundation of the future warehouse design.

---

## Warehouse Role

This dataset will become the future **FactOrderItems** table.

It will support analyses including:

- Top-selling items
- Category performance
- Product revenue
- Quantity sold
- Item popularity
- Menu mix analysis

---

## Conclusion

The Order Summary Item Report is confirmed to represent item-level business transactions.

It complements the Order Summary Report by providing detailed product information for every order.

This dataset will serve as the foundation for the FactOrderItems table within the analytical warehouse while maintaining a clear one-to-many relationship with FactOrders.

# Dataset 3 – KOT Itemwise Process Time Report

## Dataset Overview

The KOT (Kitchen Order Ticket) Itemwise Process Time Report captures the operational performance of the restaurant kitchen. Unlike the Order Summary and Order Summary Item reports, this dataset focuses on kitchen execution rather than financial transactions.

Each record contains timestamps indicating when an item entered the kitchen workflow and when its preparation was completed. This enables measurement of preparation time and overall kitchen efficiency.

### Profiler Summary

- Duplicate Rows: 0 (after Generic Silver)
- Preparation Time Range: 0.00 – 69.92 minutes
- Average Preparation Time: 16.77 minutes
- Preparation Time Median: 16.08 minutes
- Punch Time Range:
  - 01 May 2026
  - 29 June 2026

---

## Business Purpose

This dataset supports operational analytics rather than financial reporting.

Typical business questions answered include:

- Which menu items require the longest preparation time?
- What is the average kitchen preparation time?
- Which hours experience the highest kitchen workload?
- Which items consistently exceed expected preparation times?

This dataset enables restaurant managers to monitor and improve kitchen efficiency.

---

## Business Grain

### One row represents one menu item preparation event recorded for a Kitchen Order Ticket (KOT).

Each record describes the preparation of a specific menu item rather than the complete customer order.

Unlike the Order Summary datasets, this report captures operational events occurring within the kitchen after an order has been placed.

---

## Evidence

The dataset contains operational attributes that are specific to kitchen processing, including:

- KOT ID
- Item Name
- Quantity
- Punch Time
- Prepared Time
- Preparation Time Taken (mins)

The presence of both Punch Time and Prepared Time confirms that each record measures the duration of an individual preparation event rather than a financial transaction.

---

## Duplicate Analysis

The Datagami problem statement specifies that KOT reports may contain overlapping date ranges across multiple exported files.

As a result, duplicate records may exist when these files are combined.

The Generic Silver layer removes exact duplicate rows before Business Silver processing, ensuring that preparation metrics are not artificially inflated.

---

## Business Key Status

No reliable natural primary key has been identified at this stage.

Although the dataset contains KOT identifiers, uniqueness has not yet been validated across all available exports.

Following the project philosophy, no business key is assumed until warehouse modelling is completed.

---

## Measures

The following fields will become measures within the future FactKitchen table.

- Preparation Time Taken (mins)
- Quantity

These measures support operational performance analysis and kitchen efficiency reporting.

---

## Candidate Dimensions

The dataset naturally connects to the following dimensions.

- Date
- Time
- Item

Additional business dimensions such as Brand and Platform may be introduced during Business Silver if reliable relationships can be established.

---

## Business Silver Responsibilities

Business Silver will enrich this dataset where appropriate by introducing business-aware attributes such as:

- Business Date
- Weekday
- Month
- Quarter
- Daypart

Business-specific enrichment will only be performed where it can be supported by reliable relationships with the sales datasets.

---

## Warehouse Role

This dataset will become the foundation of the future **FactKitchen** table.

It will support analyses including:

- Average preparation time
- Slowest menu items
- Kitchen throughput
- Peak kitchen workload
- Preparation time by hour
- SLA breach analysis

---

## Conclusion

The KOT Itemwise Process Time Report is confirmed to represent kitchen preparation events at the item level.

Unlike the financial datasets, its primary purpose is to measure operational efficiency within the kitchen.

This dataset will provide the foundation for the FactKitchen table within the analytical warehouse.

# Proposed Warehouse Design

Based on the completed grain analysis, the warehouse will follow a Star Schema architecture.

The following fact tables have been identified:

- FactOrders
- FactOrderItems
- FactKitchen

These fact tables will be supported by shared conformed dimensions.

Proposed dimension tables include:

- DimDate
- DimBrand
- DimPlatform
- DimItem
- DimCategory
- DimOrderType
- DimPaymentType
- DimOrderStatus

Additional dimensions may be introduced if future report types provide new business entities.

# Key Architectural Decisions

The following engineering decisions have been established through this grain analysis.

## ELT Architecture

The project follows an ELT architecture where raw data is preserved in Bronze, generic cleaning is performed in Silver, business-specific enrichment is applied in Business Silver, and analytical modelling is performed in the Gold Layer.

---

## Separation of Generic and Business Logic

Generic Silver performs reusable data quality improvements such as:

- Column standardization
- Datatype conversion
- Null normalization
- Duplicate removal
- Generic validation

Business Silver introduces Restaurant POS specific transformations including:

- Brand extraction
- Platform extraction
- Brand standardization
- Platform normalization
- Business calendar derivations
- Business validations

This separation improves maintainability and reusability while preventing business rules from polluting generic transformation logic.

---

## Evidence-Based Warehouse Modelling

The warehouse design is based on observed business grain rather than assumptions.

No primary keys are assumed before understanding the datasets.

Business keys, primary keys and foreign keys will be finalized only after validating relationships during warehouse modelling.

This approach aligns with the Datagami assignment and reflects production Data Engineering practices.

---

## Star Schema

A Star Schema has been selected because it provides:

- Simple analytical queries
- Better reporting performance
- Clear separation of facts and dimensions
- Straightforward integration with Power BI

The warehouse is intended for analytical workloads rather than transactional processing.
